Bio

I am a senior undergraduate student at Colorado College, double majoring in Computer Science and Mathematics, where I work with Professor Robert Rennie on autoformalization. I've developed FORMAL, a system combining Retrieval-Augmented Thinking with agentic feedback loops that achieves 92% syntactic correctness in translating mathematics to Lean 4. I've also served as a Research Fellow with the State of the Rockies Project and interned at SocialTechLab Inc., where I engineered features for a mental wellness app.

My research interests include Formal Verification, Low-Resource NLP, and Mathematical Reasoning. Specifically, I focus on:

  • Developing resource-efficient approaches to translate between informal mathematics and formal proof systems
  • Exploring cross-lingual mathematical reasoning for underrepresented languages
  • Creating type-theoretic frameworks to enhance retrieval-augmented generation systems
